Portland, Oregon

Portland is the most populous city port within the state of oregon, founded in 1845 it is one of the most popular city for tourists to travel to because it is situated right near the pacific ocean. Therefore not only are there beautifal areas to explore but you can also check out the ocean and the lovely beaches nearby. Kyoto, Japan

Considered the heart of traditional Japanese culture, Kyoto is surrounded by shrines, and some of these shrines were created as far back as the 600's. Not only that, but long before Tokyo was established as the capital of Japan, Kyoto had been the first capital for thousands of years (literally) before Tokyo was established as the new capital of Japan and so because of it the culutral heritage surrounding the area of Kyoto is very deep and rich with history. Furthermore, alot of the old emperors of yore within Japan used to rule from within Kyoto.

Amalfi, Italy

The Amalfi coast of Italy is one of, if not the most visually stunning countrysides of Italy. The houses are built into the cliff face and look out over the ocean and are visually stunning, the people are very nice and the local cuisine is absolutely delicious if you are going to try it. A common delciacy that is often served in Amalfi is Limoncello as this is basically where Limoncello was first created.

Geneva, Switzerland

This city is most famously known for the Geneva conventions that took palce during the first WW2 and became the established protocol for dealing with POW's. Thus, the place where this meeting between political powers took place happened to be Geneva and so the convention was aptly named the Geneva conventions. It is also the second most populous city within switzerland and it is home to some five hundred thousand residents currently living there.

Washington D.C. USA

The Capital city of the U.S.A. it is home to many national monuments that were built there such as the Library of Congress, the Pentagon, The Lincoln Memorial, the Washington monument, as well as the White House. And it home to many other attractions as well that are available to see, such as an International Spy Museum. There are also other attractions that are much more grim but serve as a good reminder for us, for the soldiers who lost their lives in past wars. For instance the Tomb of the Unknown soldier is a main attraction within the D.C. area.

Yakutsk, Russia (Coldest City in the world)

Yakutsk Russia is one of the coldest cities in the world during the winter time, with temperatures regularly dropping to below -50°C in the middle of the winter, and its insane to think about but during the summer the city manages to warm back up again to around a nice comfortable 25-29°C weather. It's currently home to around three hundred thousand residents and the entire megacity is bult on permafrost which is really cool to think about.
